Ukraine Is Not a Brothel is a 2013 Australian documentary film directed by Kitty Green. The film debuted at the 70th Venice International Film Festival, although it was not part of the competition.[1] The documentary concerns the FEMEN (Фемен) movement, a feminist protest group originating from Ukraine.[2]The film is a documentary on the activities of FEMEN, a Ukrainian feminist movement known for topless protests. The filmmaker Kitty Green[3][4] followed the activists for over a year, in Ukraine, Belarus and Turkey.[5] It documents the protests and the resulting harassments and arrests[6] over a span of fourteen months.[7] The film showcases the four activists who form the core of the film.[8]The documentary shows Viktor Sviatsky as a driving force of the group FEMEN.
